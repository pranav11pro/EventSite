All attendees, speakers, and event organizers at Online 500 Canva Workshop are required to adhere to the following Code of Conduct. Event organizers will enforce this Code throughout the event entirely. Prior to the first Virtual meeting, all participants are expected to be familiar with this policy.

Value

We respect everyone’s identity, opinion, and characteristics.

Our goal is to create a welcoming and inclusive environment for all participants.

Seek out ideas from event organizers and participants with different experiences, perspectives and backgrounds.

Discrimination

Discrimination based on sex, race, religion, nationality or disability will be investigated and dealt with accordingly.

Harassment

Cyberstalking is a type of online harassment given our platform of Zoom and Microsoft Teams to stalk a victim. There is generally a pattern of threatening or malicious behaviors. Wisconsin has an anti-stalking law and all matters deemed as cyberstalking will be thoroughly investigated and reported to police.

Our organization prohibits any sort of threatening or harassing emails, messages, or posting of information online that targets a specific person causing them fear or anger and will be immediately handled. It will be published and can no longer be erased, and the Wisconsin anti-stalking law will be referred to.

Threatening, intimidating, stereotyping, using slurs, violence, and all other hostile acts that cause discomfort to your fellow attendees and organizers will not be tolerated.

Sexual harassment including unwelcome requests for sexual favors or verbal conduct of a sexual nature including sexual jokes, graphic spoken commentary about a person’s body, derogatory or degrading remarks, will be investigated.

Reporting misconduct

Any suspected misconduct reported will be dealt with and can be reported anonymously.

All upper-level staff are mandated reporters and can be informed of any misconduct.

Conduct can be reported through a mandated reporter.

Address and report inappropriate behavior and comments that are discriminatory, harassing, offensive, unwelcome, or abusive.

Tardiness/absenteeism

Unjustified absences in the event won’t be appreciated, and may result in exclusion from the program and future events.

Technology abuse

To protect all parties involved, recordings not made by the organizers will not be allowed to be created or distributed.

Personal information such as names, and audio recordings will not be used for purposes other than educational.

Meeting links and passcodes for Zoom meetings will be sent personally via email. Sharing or forwarding of this information will result in immediate expulsion from the event.

Respect

Treat others with dignity and courtesy at all times.

Communication

Be considerate and respectful in your speech and actions.

Everyone must have an equal opportunity to talk, there should be a welcoming environment that allows open communication.

Fair Use

Plagiarizing the work of your peers or others is not allowed. All submitted works that are not done by you must be referenced. If event organizers view submitted work as plagiarism there will be a further investigation with the participant(s).
